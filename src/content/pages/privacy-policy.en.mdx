## Overview

ImageDPIChanger is a free image DPI metadata tool available at ImageDPIChanger.com. It is designed for a narrow purpose: helping you change DPI/PPI metadata in supported image files without creating an account, paying for a plan, or uploading your images to our servers.

This Privacy Policy explains what ImageDPIChanger does and does not collect when you use the public image DPI changer. It applies to ImageDPIChanger.com and not to websites, apps, or services that we do not operate.

## What ImageDPIChanger Does

ImageDPIChanger changes metadata that tells software how to interpret an image's print density. For supported PNG and JPEG files, the tool updates DPI-related metadata in your browser and prepares a corrected download on the same device.

Changing DPI metadata does not add pixels, improve image quality, or turn a low-resolution file into a high-resolution file. It only changes the metadata that many printers, document systems, and submission portals check.

## Our Privacy Position

The public ImageDPIChanger tool is built to avoid personal-data collection wherever possible.

- You do not need an account to use the DPI changer.
- You do not need to provide your name, email address, phone number, billing details, or profile information.
- We do not charge for the public DPI changer.
- We do not ask you to upload original images or converted images.
- We do not sell, rent, or share personal information for advertising.

If ImageDPIChanger later adds optional accounts, support forms, analytics, paid features, or any other feature that changes these practices, this policy should be updated before those features collect related information.

## Your Images and Conversion Data

When you select an image in the public DPI changer, your browser reads the file locally. The conversion happens in browser memory, and the converted file is generated for download on your device.

ImageDPIChanger does not intentionally receive, upload, store, inspect, or keep copies of:

- your original images;
- your converted images;
- image filenames beyond what your own browser displays during the session;
- DPI values you choose for a specific file;
- conversion history or download history.

You retain all rights to your images. Because the public tool does not upload your files to ImageDPIChanger, we do not claim ownership of them and do not receive a license to use them.
